Writing my first blog post was quite confusing. I spent a decent amount of time debating whether it should be about a hobby project, a paper review, or something else technical. In the end, I decided to start with a personal post. I would briefly share my overall journey, with a slightly more detailed look at what I did over the past year.

Of all these phases of life, the two I cherish most are my years at BITS Goa and at CERN. In retrospect, both were shaped by the people I met there. But let's move on to the past year.

In 2025, I applied for a master's in computer science for the September intake to continue my studies, but unfortunately, my subpar grades (I actually think they are quite okay, tbh) got me rejected by the places I wanted to go. Since the rejections came so close to the end of my contract at CERN, I started a desperate search for jobs in Europe. I continued my job search after my return to India until December, without any luck. 

In December, I realized I had wanted to study all along, so I began another search for a master's program. I applied to a few other places and was accepted into a couple of excellent programs. However, I was hesitant since they were expensive and I hadn’t saved enough. As of now, I have decided to join IMT Atlantique's MS in Data Science program, and I am grateful and excited for this next step.

In February, I realized that throughout this time, I had been under a lot of stress, building in the background since I was a kid. The competitive education system, the pressure for good grades in university, the pressure to find a job after university, and the anxiety that had been mounting after CERN all contributed. This ongoing pressure made me quite miserable for the people around me and for myself. After CERN, I let it affect my relationships with people I cared about.  

I started to realize how haphazard and chaotic my mind had been, and how much it had affected the people around me. After this, I resolved that before moving on with my life, I would try to understand what was going on, as I was also fed up with being apologetic for myself. These are some of the things that had a strong impact on me -

1. Scattered Minds by Gabor Maté - This is a book I don’t remember how I came across, but something about the title stuck with me. It took me a couple of months to get through it because it was one of the most emotionally heavy reads of all time for me. It showed me an image of myself that I realized I had been running away from. I always had a problem with the label of ADD, as it often trivialized the subjective experience to simply a lack of attention and its consequent symptoms. Gabor Maté’s holistic viewpoint provided much more intuitive clarity than anything I had come across up to that point.
2. Quitting Nicotine – I had been smoking, vaping, chewing, and whatnot for about eight years. After three or four years, I already knew it was harmful. Only recently did I notice its effects on my mind and how it enslaves it. At the suggestion of a friend, I started with Allen Carr’s book on quitting nicotine. While reading it, I understood that the book uses repetition to reverse the “brainwash” that got me hooked in the first place. Even knowing this, I kept reading because I truly wanted it to work. Now, after several months, I have not touched nicotine. I am glad to report that I feel much better without it.
3. Vipassana 10-Day Meditation Camp - Very late, I realized that my family has a deep connection with yoga and meditation. A couple of my cousins had attended the Vipassana camp and trusted in its positive impact. The idea of spending 10 hours a day meditating for 10 days, without a phone or outside contact, was very appealing to me. I attended my camp in Pushkar, Rajasthan, one of the most beautiful places I’ve seen, yet it was also one of the most grueling experiences of my life. Those 10 days had a powerful impact, helping me see my inner reality, both good and bad. This camp marked the start of my inward journey to understand and accept my experiences. I could recount much more about those 10 days, but I will keep it short: it was exactly what I needed during a stressful time in my life.
4. Level 1 Yoga TTC - I started this for multiple reasons. Firstly, I was very curious whether it could help me live a better life. Secondly, I wanted to become more flexible, as I also do freestyle dancing, and I could see the benefits yoga would have. Thirdly, I would also like to teach it to my friends and family, so I am doing the TTC (Teacher Training). Currently, I am halfway through my course, and I already feel it has made my body a better place to be.

I should also add that what helped me goes beyond these examples. Countless other small things contributed positively to my mental health after my burnout. I cannot thank my family and friends enough for all the support they gave me, because none of the above would have amounted to much without them.

Now, turning to a more positive note, I would like to mark this post as a new beginning or a fresh start (it feels more like a change than a clean slate, tbh). My mind is still chaotic, and my ways remain haphazard, but I have come to terms with them, understanding that this is a long journey with myself. With this, I want to say I am incredibly grateful to everyone in my life. Moving forward, my goal is to take care of my mental health through aligning my priorities and treasuring the people I care about :)
